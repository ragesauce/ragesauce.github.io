What this does & doesn't. It encrypts for confidentiality — the recipient decrypts with their private key. It does not sign, so it carries no proof of who wrote it (signing needs your private key, which has no business being pasted into a web page). Decrypt with gpg -d, or any OpenPGP client.

Trust model. Encryption is computed locally via OpenPGP.js. No network calls are made with your data. The library itself loads from a public CDN; for true air-gapped use, save this file and vendor the script locally. Crypto is only as strong as your verification that the key belongs to who you think it does — hence the fingerprint above.
